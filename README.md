# Restaurant Operations & Performance Dashboard

This project analyses transactional data from a 3-branch restaurant chain to evaluate revenue performance, customer behaviour, and operational efficiency.

The goal of this dashboard is to move beyond basic reporting and provide actionable insights that can support decision-making around sales, pricing, and operations.

---

## Dataset
- **Source:** [Serayu Chicken : Restraurant Sales (3 Years) - Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/bertnardomariouskono/serayu-chicken-time-series-and-forecasting-3-yrs )
- ~626,000 transactions  
- Time period: Jan 2023 – Dec 2025  
- Data includes:
  - Sales amount  
  - Product categories  
  - Order type (Dine-in, Takeaway, Online)  
  - Payment methods  
  - Branch-level performance

## Key Metrics
- Total Revenue: 12.5bn IDR  
- Total Orders: 206K  
- Average Order Value (AOV): 58.4K  
- Estimated Profit Margin: ~25% (based on industry assumptions)  
- Revenue Growth: ~40% over 3 years  

Note: Profit is estimated using industry-standard cost assumptions due to lack of cost data.

---

## Key Insights

1. Strong Business Growth  
Revenue increased by ~40% over three years, showing consistent growth and stable demand.

2. Balanced Branch Performance  
All three branches contribute evenly to revenue, indicating operational consistency.

3. High Dependence on Dine-In  
Dine-in contributes ~50% of total revenue, making the business reliant on foot traffic.

4. Food Dominates Revenue  
Food accounts for ~80% of revenue, highlighting missed upselling opportunities in drinks.

5. Revenue Concentration in Top Products  
A small number of products generate a large share of revenue.

6. Pricing & Volume Imbalance  
Some high-volume items generate relatively low revenue.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Increase takeaway and online sales through promotions  
- Introduce combo meals to improve drink sales  
- Promote second-tier products to reduce dependency  
- Review pricing strategy for high-volume items  
- Standardise successful strategies across branches
